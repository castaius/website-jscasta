# New City Growth System

This is the starting operating system for building Jscasta in a new city with no local client base yet.

## Starting Reality

The challenge is not lack of ability. The challenge is lack of local trust.

Past TV appearances, brand work, and international credits still matter, but they cannot be the whole sales argument if the new market does not recognize the names or language. They need to be translated into practical proof:

- "Companies have trusted me with real audiences."
- "I can handle pressure."
- "I am professional before, during, and after the event."
- "Guests will be engaged, surprised, and talking about it afterward."
- "Booking me will be easy and low-risk."

## Positioning For The Website

Lead with the buyer's problem, then support it with proof.

Possible core angle:

> Modern close-up and event magic for hosts who want guests talking, laughing, and fully present.

Avoid relying only on "as seen on TV" or foreign brand names. Use them lower on the page as credibility, but make the first screen answer:

- What do you do?
- Who do you serve in this city?
- What problem do you solve?
- How does someone inquire?
- Why should they trust you if they do not know you yet?

## The First Three Offers

Start with simple offers that are easy to understand and easy to sell.

1. Private Party Close-Up Magic
   - For birthdays, house parties, anniversaries, dinners, and celebrations.
   - Promise: guests mix faster, awkward silences disappear, and the host gets a memorable feature without needing a stage.

2. Corporate Mix-And-Mingle Magic
   - For networking events, holiday parties, client nights, team events, launches, and receptions.
   - Promise: creates interaction, gives people something to talk about, and makes the event feel polished.

3. Restaurant / Venue Guest Experience
   - For restaurants, bars, hotels, and lounges.
   - Promise: improves dwell time, guest memory, reviews, and repeat visits.

Do not start with too many show types. Three clear doors are enough.

## Website Minimum Viable System

The site needs to become a lead-capture machine before it becomes a perfect portfolio.

Minimum pages:

- Home
- Private Parties
- Corporate Events
- Restaurants / Venues
- About
- Contact

Minimum trust assets:

- Short performance reel or embedded clips.
- 3-5 strong photos.
- Testimonials, even if translated or from previous markets.
- Brand / media credits reframed as "Selected past audiences and appearances."
- A simple FAQ that reduces booking anxiety.
- Clear local city language across the site.

Minimum contact form fields:

- Name
- Email
- Phone
- Event type
- Event date
- Event location
- Estimated guest count
- Budget range
- Short event description

## CRM And Follow-Up System

Every inquiry should enter one tracker immediately, even before a full CRM is chosen.

Start with a spreadsheet if needed. Track:

- Date inquiry received
- Name
- Email
- Phone
- Event type
- Event date
- Location
- Lead source
- Quoted price
- Status
- Follow-up date
- Outcome
- Notes

Follow-up rhythm:

- Within 1 hour: personal reply or call.
- Same day: send package/next step.
- 2 days later: follow up if no response.
- 7 days later: follow up again.
- 30 days later: soft check-in.
- 1 year later: reactivation for birthdays, annual events, and holiday parties.

## First 30 Days

Week 1: Build the foundation.

- Decide the main city and surrounding service area.
- Choose the first three offers.
- Create the minimum website pages.
- Add a clear inquiry form.
- Create a lead tracker.
- Write reply templates for each offer.
- Collect all existing photos, video, testimonials, logos, and press into one folder.

Week 2: Create local proof.

- Offer 3-5 strategic free or discounted appearances only where they can produce useful proof.
- Good targets: charity events, coworking spaces, networking groups, restaurants, hotel lounges, community events, open houses.
- Requirement: get photos, video clips, testimonial, and permission to use them.
- Do not do vague free work. Every appearance must create proof or a relationship.

Week 3: Start outreach.

- Build a list of 100 local targets:
  - 25 event planners
  - 25 restaurants/bars/hotels
  - 20 coworking spaces/networking groups
  - 15 corporate offices or HR/event contacts
  - 15 private event venues
- Send a short, specific message to each.
- Follow up once after 4-7 days.
- Track every contact.

Week 4: Publish local search content.

- Create 8 local pages or posts:
  - Magician in [City]
  - Close-up magician in [City]
  - Corporate magician in [City]
  - Private party magician in [City]
  - Restaurant magician in [City]
  - Entertainment ideas for [City] corporate events
  - Unique private party entertainment in [City]
  - How to hire a magician in [City]

I would love to send you a short clip and see if this could be useful for anything you have coming up.

Best,
Jscasta

## First Success Metrics

Track these weekly:

- New local contacts added
- Outreach messages sent
- Replies received
- Calls booked
- Inquiries from website
- Quotes sent
- Bookings closed
- Testimonials collected
- Local content pages published

The first goal is not immediate fame. The first goal is movement: conversations, local proof, and a repeatable booking path.

## Guiding Principle

In a new city, do not try to convince people that your past reputation should matter.

Show them that hiring you is easy, safe, professional, and memorable now.
