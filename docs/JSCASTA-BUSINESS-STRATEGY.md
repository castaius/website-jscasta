# JSCASTA Business Strategy

## Current Situation

JSCASTA is an experienced international magician, but is currently rebuilding his client base in Sydney, Australia.

The business currently has:

- Strong international performance experience
- More than 17 years in professional magic
- Work with global brands
- Experience in television, festivals, corporate events and content
- A psychology background
- An existing website that is incomplete
- Very limited local clients, connections and Australian proof

International credentials are useful supporting evidence, but they cannot be the entire sales proposition because many local buyers will not recognize the television appearances, brands or Spanish-language work.

The immediate challenge is to create a functioning local client acquisition system.

## Strategic Principle

The business should build a complete and repeatable client journey rather than treating the website, CRM, content and outreach as unrelated projects.

The intended journey is:

Visitor or prospect -> focused landing page -> inquiry form -> CRM -> fast personal response -> discovery call -> proposal -> contract -> payment -> event preparation -> performance -> review -> referral and future follow-up

The website is the front door of this system.

## Initial Market Focus

The first target market is corporate events in Sydney.

Primary buyers:

- Event agency producers
- Corporate event planners
- Executive assistants
- Marketing managers
- People and Culture managers
- Conference producers
- Hotel and venue event coordinators

## Initial Offer

Interactive roving magic for:

- Corporate cocktail receptions
- Networking events
- Conferences
- Awards nights
- Company celebrations
- Client functions

Typical performance duration:

- 60 to 90 minutes

## Problem Being Solved

Corporate event organizers need to:

- Energize a room
- Help guests begin conversations
- Reduce awkward networking periods
- Create memorable moments
- Maintain energy between programmed parts of an event
- Offer entertainment that is flexible and technically simple

## Experience Provided

JSCASTA moves through the event performing short, interactive pieces of magic for small groups.

The performance combines:

- Psychology
- Humour
- Curiosity
- Storytelling
- Impossible moments
- Respectful audience interaction

The experience should help guests connect and give them something to continue discussing throughout the event.

## Positioning

The brand should feel:

- Intelligent
- Warm
- Curious
- Premium
- Playful
- Human
- Internationally experienced

Avoid:

- Generic magician cliches
- Excessive mystery language
- Fake luxury language
- Exaggerated claims
- Tiny text
- Visual clutter
- Excessive animation
- Generic stock imagery
- Multiple competing calls to action
- The rhetorical structure "it's not X, it's Y"

## Credibility

Relevant proof includes:

- More than 17 years of professional experience
- Performances across Latin America, North America, Europe and Australia
- Work with dozens of brands
- Selected clients including Google, Coca-Cola, Sony Music Entertainment and Warner Bros. Pictures
- Formal education in psychology
- Graduation from the Chavez Studio of Magic

Credentials should support the offer rather than dominate the homepage.

## Pricing Direction

The current internal minimum being considered for a local corporate engagement is approximately AUD $1,000.

This is not yet final public pricing and should not automatically be added to the website unless explicitly requested.

## Current Website Objective

The first website milestone is not a complete multi-page website.

It is a focused minimum viable sales page that allows a corporate buyer to:

1. Understand the offer within ten seconds.
2. See what result the entertainment creates.
3. See relevant video or photographic proof.
4. Understand where the service fits.
5. Trust that JSCASTA is experienced and professional.
6. Submit an inquiry easily.

## Minimum Page Structure

1. Hero section
2. Clear event outcome
3. Performance video or showreel
4. Explanation of how roving magic works
5. Suitable event types
6. Benefits for the organizer and guests
7. Credibility and selected clients
8. Testimonial or proof
9. Frequently asked questions
10. Inquiry call to action and form

## Primary Call To Action

"Tell me about your event"

This should be the dominant conversion action.

## Current Implementation Phase
